My research examines the psychological mechanisms linking social identity, 
masking, and mental health outcomes in autistic adults. I work within a 
neurodiversity paradigm and a social model of disability, using mixed methods 
(PLS-SEM, reflexive thematic analysis, conceptual content analysis) and 
participatory frameworks. I am particularly attentive to the experiences of 
autistic women and gender minorities, who remain underrepresented in both 
research and clinical practice.

## Research Themes

- Social camouflaging: cognitive and psychosocial predictors, identity costs
- Autistic burnout: conceptualization, severity, and measurement
- Minority stress, mental health, and intersectionality in autistic adults
- Participatory and community-based research methodologies
- Gender diversity and neurodevelopmental conditions

## Current Projects

**Validation of the ABSI-Fr**  
The Autistic Burnout Severity Items (French adaptation) is currently undergoing 
psychometric validation. Data collection is ongoing.  
*Participants can express interest by contacting me directly.*

**Science outreach on autism and social camouflage**  
I organize cinema-debate events for general and practitioner audiences, 
combining film screenings with structured discussion on autism, camouflaging, 
and mental health.

**Video capsule on autistic burnout**  
In collaboration with [Hogrefe](#), I am developing an educational video 
resource on autistic burnout aimed at clinicians and researchers.

## Future Directions

I am interested in pursuing postdoctoral research at the intersection of 
gender diversity, minority stress, and autistic burnout, with a focus on 
participatory methodologies and the development of clinically relevant tools. 
I maintain active collaborations with researchers in Australia and Canada and 
am open to international opportunities.
