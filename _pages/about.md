## About Me

I am a Teaching and Research Associate (ATER) at the 
[Université Toulouse Jean Jaurès](https://www.univ-tlse2.fr/) and a 
postdoctoral applicant in clinical psychology. I completed my PhD in 2025 
under the supervision of Pr. Jeanne Kruck, with a dissertation examining 
social camouflaging as a risk factor for autistic burnout, combining 
structural equation modeling and qualitative methods with autistic adults.

My work sits at the intersection of clinical psychology, disability studies, 
and participatory research. I am committed to neurodiversity-affirmative 
science and to centering the lived experience of autistic people, including 
gender minorities and women, in both research design and interpretation. 
As a late-diagnosed autistic and ADHD researcher, I approach these questions 
as both a scientist and an insider.

I am currently seeking a postdoctoral position starting September 2026 or 
2027, in the UK, Europe, Canada, or Australia.

---

## Research Interests

- Autistic burnout: predictors, mechanisms, maintenance, and recovery
- Social camouflaging as a risk factor: psychosocial and cognitive predictors
- Mental health in autism: anxiety, depression, trauma, and differential diagnosis
- Minority stress, internalized stigma, and autistic identity
- Gender minorities and intersectional approaches to autism research
- Alexithymia, executive functioning, and emotional processing
- Participatory and mixed-methods research with autistic communities

## Current Projects

**Completing the ABSI-Fr validation**
I am currently finalizing the French adaptation and psychometric validation 
of the Autistic Burnout Severity Items (ABSI-Fr), in collaboration with 
the original authors at the University of New South Wales. Autistic adults 
living in France are warmly invited to 
[participate in the study](https://enquetes.univ-tlse2.fr/index.php/199918?lang=fr).

**Completing manuscripts from doctoral research**
I am finalizing several articles from my dissertation, including a qualitative 
study on recovery strategies following autistic burnout and an investigation 
of psychosocial and environmental risk factors for burnout severity.

**Upcoming: INSAR 2026**
I will be presenting at the International Society for Autism Research Annual 
Meeting (Prague, April 2026): *Exploring Predictors of Autistic Burnout 
Severity in Adults: Individual, Interpersonal, and Structural Factors.*
