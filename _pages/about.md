# About Me

I am a Teaching and Research Associate (ATER) at the [Université Toulouse Jean Jaurès](https://www.univ-tlse2.fr/) and a postdoctoral applicant in developmental psychology. I completed my PhD in 2025 under the supervision of Dr. Jeanne Kruck, with a dissertation examining social camouflaging as a risk factor for autistic burnout, combining structural equation modeling and qualitative methods.

My work sits at the intersection of clinical psychology, disability studies, and participatory research. I am committed to neurodiversity-affirmative science and to centering the lived experience of autistic people — including gender minorities and women — in both research design and interpretation. As a late-diagnosed autistic and ADHD researcher, I approach these questions as both a scientist and an insider.

I am currently based in Toulouse, France, and actively seeking postdoctoral opportunities to expand my work on autistic mental health internationally.

# Research

My research focuses on the psychological mechanisms that connect social identity, camouflaging, and mental health outcomes in autistic adults. I work within a neurodiversity paradigm and a social model of disability, using mixed methods (PLS-SEM, reflexive thematic analysis, conceptual content analysis) and participatory frameworks.

**Current themes:**
- Social camouflaging and its cognitive and psychosocial predictors
- Autistic burnout: conceptualization, measurement, and severity
- Minority stress and mental health in autistic gender minorities
- Participatory and community-based research methodologies

**Current projects:**
- Validation of the ABSI-Fr (Autistic Burnout Severity Index, French adaptation), currently in data collection phase — [participate here](#)
- Science outreach on autism and social camouflage (cinema-debate format, practitioner audiences)
- Video capsule on autistic burnout for [Hogrefe](#)

I maintain active collaborations with researchers in Australia and Canada, with a long-term interest in the intersection of gender diversity and autistic burnout.
